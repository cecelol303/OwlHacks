What's an STD?

An STD (Sexually Transmitted Disease) is an infection that is spread primarily through sexual contact. STDs can be caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Common modes of transmission include vaginal, anal, and oral sex, but some can also be spread through skin-to-skin contact or from mother to child during childbirth. Some common STDs include: • Chlamydia (bacterial) • Gonorrhea (bacterial) • Syphilis (bacterial) • Human Papillomavirus (HPV) (viral) • Herpes Simplex Virus (HSV) (viral) • HIV/AIDS (viral) • Trichomoniasis (parasitic) . Symptoms can vary depending on the STD, but many STDs may not cause noticeable symptoms initially, which can lead to delayed treatment. Untreated STDs can lead to more serious health issues, such as infertility, organ damage, or increased risk of HIV.

Treatment

Testing and treatment are available for most STDs, with bacterial and parasitic infections typically being curable with antibiotics or antiparasitics, while viral infections can be managed but not always cured. Practicing safe sex, such as using condoms, is one of the best ways to reduce the risk of contracting an STD.
